The problem

"Wait until they're ready" is the most expensive advice in motherhood.

You know these nights. You have lived these afternoons.

  • Bedtime is a 45-minute negotiation that ends with someone crying. Some nights it is not the toddler.
  • You have left a restaurant before the entrees arrived, carrying a screaming child past tables of people pretending not to look.
  • You are on your third potty chart. The stickers work for four days. Then they do not.
  • You Google "is this normal" at midnight and get seventeen contradictory answers from strangers.

None of this means your kid is broken, and it does not mean you are failing. It means you were handed advice built on waiting: wait until they show signs, wait until they want to, wait until they are ready.

"While you wait, the easy window quietly closes, and the thing that could have been simple at sixteen months becomes a battle at three." From the introduction, Foundation Before Freedom

The stakes are real. Every month spent waiting is a month the window narrows. The tantrum gets more practiced. The pattern gets more permanent. And you get more tired.
